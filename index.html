India

February 15 - 25

The first half of my time in India was spent celebrating a friend’s wedding. Traditional Indian weddings last for 3 days at least. Some days had multiple events at different locations. Thank god for Uber is all I can say. I don’t know how I would have gotten from point A to B to C and back to B. Every event included a crazy amount of food and open bar. This open bar didn’t contain the cheap stuff, btw. It’s difficult to sum up 4 days of events, but you can check out some of the festivities in this video I created of my time in India.

After the wedding festivities were over, I did what all good tourists should do while visiting India; visit the Taj Mahal (pictured above). All my life I’ve seen this structure in text books, wondering if I’d ever get to see it in real life. Let me tell you something, folks: the photos in text books do not do this place justice. The Taj Mahal did not meet my expectations, it exceeded it tenfold. In addition to being awed by the Taj Majal, I visited Humayun's Tomb, Old Delhi, and other historical sites Delhi has to offer.

While in Poland I split my time between Warsaw and Krakow, what most tourists consider the two main cities of the country. I have always loved history and was so excited for the chance to learn more about Eastern Europe and Poland specifically.
